Katherine Johnson

The Woman Who Calculated the Stars

The Incredible Journey of Katherine Johnson

  • August 26, 1918: Born in White Sulphur Springs, West Virginia.
  • 1928: Completed eighth grade at age 10.
  • 1929: Began attending high school at age 10.
  • 1936: Graduated summa cum laude from West Virginia State College with degrees in mathematics and French.
  • 1939: Selected as one of the first African American students to enroll in graduate studies at West Virginia University.
  • 1939: Married James Goble.
  • 1953: Joined the National Advisory Committee for Aeronautics (NACA), which later became NASA, as a "computer."
  • 1956: Her husband, James Goble, passed away.
  • 1959: Married James A. Johnson.
  • 1961: Calculated the trajectory for Alan Shepard's Freedom 7 mission, the first American in space.
  • 1962: Verified the calculations for John Glenn's orbit around Earth, at Glenn's personal request.
  • 1969: Contributed to the trajectory calculations for the Apollo 11 mission, enabling the first Moon landing.
  • 1970: Provided critical calculations that helped guide the Apollo 13 astronauts safely back to Earth after an oxygen tank explosion aborted their mission.
  • 1986: Retired from NASA after 33 years of service.
  • 1999: Named Outstanding Alumnus of the Year by West Virginia State College.
  • 2015: Awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom by President Barack Obama.
  • 2016: The film "Hidden Figures" was released, highlighting her contributions to NASA and space exploration.
  • 2017: NASA's Langley Research Center dedicated the Katherine G. Johnson Computational Research Facility in her honor.
  • 2019: Awarded the Congressional Gold Medal.
  • February 24, 2020: Passed away at the age of 101.
